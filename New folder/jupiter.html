Jupiter :-

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, and is made up of mostly hydrogen and helium. It's known as the "king of the planets" because it's named after the Roman god Jupiter. Features Great Red Spot A giant, red-orange storm in Jupiter's atmosphere that's twice the width of Earth Rings Jupiter has a simple ring system with an inner halo, a main ring, and a Gossamer ring Magnetic field Jupiter's magnetic field is 16 to 54 times stronger than Earth's, and extends to Saturn's orbit Aurorae Jupiter's magnetic field causes some of the most spectacular aurorae in the solar system Other facts Jupiter is the fifth planet from the sun Jupiter rotates once every 10 hours, which is about 2.5 times faster than Earth Jupiter takes nearly 12 Earth years to orbit the sun Jupiter's atmosphere is made up of hydrogen, helium, water droplets, ice crystals, ammonia crystals, and other elements Jupiter is visible throughout the year, but can be difficult to spot when it's too close to the sun Exploration The NASA spacecraft Juno has been studying Jupiter and its moons since 2016.
